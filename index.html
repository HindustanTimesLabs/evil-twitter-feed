What’s it like to get trolled all day long?

By Harry Stevens and Piyush Aggarwal
17 April, 2017 | #LetsTalkAboutTrolls »

Sometimes, being an outspoken woman on Twitter is hard. To find out how hard, we gathered a day’s worth of tweets sent to four prominent Indian women — Rana Ayyuab, Tavleen Singh, Barkha Dutt, and Madhu Kishwar. We then filtered those tweets for profane or abusive words, categorized them, and sped them up by a thousand times. Now you, too, can experience the torrent of vitriol unleashed upon these women over the course of a day.

Warning: The following contains coarse language and may not be suitable for children or those offended by profanity.

As you can see, some of the terms we’ve highlighted are not always used abusively. Sometimes they are even used supportively. Other terms are not abusive in and of themselves — Pakistani, for example, is just a nationality — but are used so frequently in abusive ways that we’ve included them here.

For Ayyub, a Muslim, hateful tweets are often coloured by Islamophobia. Trolls like to pretend Ayyub is an agent of Pakistan’s Inter-Services Intelligence (ISI) and to demand she “return” to Pakistan. Any criticism of India or its government, however reasonable or constructive, may be deemed traitorous.

Twitter users who are unable or unwilling to engage with the content of Dutt’s reporting often resort, as a child might, to name calling. Dutt must frequently endure sexist and racist slurs and violent threats. Trolls also accuse her of being a shill for political causes, typically left-wing ones.

The trolls are not quite as vicious to Dutt as they are to Ayyub — on February 28, we flagged about twice as many abusive tweets sent to Ayyub — but their tweets can still be appalling.

A note on our methodology

These tweets were collected on the evening of April 17, 2017. We collected as many tweet mentions as possible for all four women, and then selected the day with the highest minimum tagged tweets among the four. That day was TKTKTKT, when TKTKTK had TKTKTK tagged tweets.
